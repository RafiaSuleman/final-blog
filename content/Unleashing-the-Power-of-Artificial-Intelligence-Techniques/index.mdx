 ###   2. Deep learning
Deep learning, a subtype of machine learning, uses deep neural networks, which include several layers. Ever wondered how Siri can recognize your voice? Image and speech recognition are examples of Deep learning. 
### 3. Natural Language Processing
This method enables machines to understand spoken or written text, and consequently respond in a human-like manner. Applications incorporate language interpretation, opinion examination, and chatbots.
### 4. Computer Vision
Images and videos are processed and understood by computer vision algorithms. Object location, facial acknowledgment, and independent vehicles are normal applications.
### 5. Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)
Time series prediction, speech recognition, and language modeling make use of RNNs, which are specialized neural networks designed for data sequences
### 6. Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs):
CNNs have played a crucial role in the development of computer vision and are intended for image-processing tasks. They use convolutional layers to consequently distinguish designs in pictures.
### 7. Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs):
GANs comprise two neural networks, a generator, and a discriminator, which contend with one another. They are utilized for creating sensible information, like pictures, sound, and text.
### 8. Transfer Learning
Transfer learning includes reusing pre-trained models on new, related errands. This can save a ton of time and computational assets in preparing computer-based intelligence models.
### 9.     Evolutionary Algorithms
These algorithms imitate the method of natural selection to improve answers for challenging issues. They take part in search and optimization duties.
### 10.  Expert Systems
Master frameworks use rule-based information portrayal to tackle issues in unambiguous spaces, frequently including dynamics in light of specialist knowledge.
### 11.  Swarm Intelligence
Swarm intelligence methods, such as particle swarm optimization, are used for search and optimization tasks. These algorithms are motivated by the behavior of social organisms.
### 12.  Quantum Computing
Quantum computing is a new field that uses quantum mechanics to solve troublesome issues, such as cryptography and AI tasks like optimization.
### 13.  Explainable AI (XAI)
The goal of XAI techniques is to make AI models easier to understand and use, making it easier for humans to understand why AI makes decisions.
### 14.  Fuzzy Logic
Fuzzy logic is helpful in control systems and decision-making because it enables AI systems to handle uncertainty and imprecision.
### 15.  Bayesian Networks
Bayesian networks are used in risk analysis and decision support systems to model probabilistic relationships between variables.
